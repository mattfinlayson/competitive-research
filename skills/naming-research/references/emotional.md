# Emotional Associations Reference

How names evoke emotions and perceptions.

## Overview

Names aren't just identifiers—they trigger emotional responses. This reference helps analyze and test emotional associations.

## The 3-Word Test

For each candidate name, ask 5+ people outside the project:

> "If [NAME] were a person, what 3 words describe them?"

Record the responses and look for patterns.

## Common Association Categories

### Trust & Reliability

Words that signal dependability:
- Trustworthy, Reliable, Professional
- Official, Certified, Verified
- Secure, Safe, Protected

**Use for**: Compliance, security, finance products

### Innovation & Modernity

Words that signal progress:
- Modern, Innovative, Cutting-edge
- Smart, Intelligent, Advanced
- Fresh, New, Next-generation

**Use for**: Tech products, SaaS, platforms

### Approachability & Friendliness

Words that signal ease:
- Friendly, Easy, Approachable
- Simple, Clear, Intuitive
- Helpful, Supportive, Warm

**Use for**: SMB products, consumer apps

### Enterprise & Serious

Words that signal professionalism:
- Professional, Corporate, Enterprise
- Sophisticated, Mature, Established
- Comprehensive, Robust, Powerful

**Use for**: Enterprise products, B2B

### Fun & Playful

Words that signal personality:
- Fun, Playful, Quirky
- Creative, Artistic, Expressive
- Bold, Brave, Adventurous

**Use for**: Consumer products, creative tools

## Scoring Rubric

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Strong positive associations, clear emotional fit with target market |
| 4 | Positive associations, minor misalignments |
| 3 | Neutral or mixed associations |
| 2 | Some negative associations, notable misalignment |
| 1 | Strong negative associations or confusion |

## The 5+ People Test

### Methodology

1. **Recruit participants**: 5+ people outside the project
2. **Provide context**: "This is a name for a [type of product]"
3. **Ask the 3-word test**: Record responses
4. **Look for patterns**: Common words across participants
5. **Check alignment**: Do associations match brand positioning?

### Questions to Ask

1. What 3 words describe this name?
2. What kind of company would use this name?
3. Would you trust a product with this name?
4. Does it feel modern or dated?
5. Is it memorable?

### Analysis

**Strong associations** (5+ people say similar words):
- Good: Clear emotional positioning
- Action: Capitalize on the associations

**Mixed associations** (wide variance):
- Caution: Unclear positioning
- Action: Investigate why variance exists

**Negative associations** (same negative words):
- Problem: Name conflicts with positioning
- Action: Consider alternatives

## Common Emotional Pitfalls

### Too Boring

**Problem**: Name evokes no emotion

**Examples**: ProcessLog, DataTrack, SubPro

**Fix**: Add evocative element

### Too Playful

**Problem**: Name doesn't convey seriousness

**Examples**: TrustyPanda, FunCompliance

**Fix**: Add professional element

### Too Technical

**Problem**: Name alienates non-technical users

**Examples**: GDPRPro, SubProcessorMax

**Fix**: Abstract beyond technical terms

### Inconsistent

**Problem**: Name means different things to different people

**Examples**: Most abstract names initially

**Fix**: Build association through marketing
